1. Introduction

Welcome to Share Eat, a mobile platform that connects Foodie Content Creators with Restaurants for User Generated Content (UGC) marketing collaborations. Share Eat is operated by Share Eat Sdn. Bhd. (hereinafter referred to as "we", "our", or "us").

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, disclose, and protect your personal data when you access and use the Share Eat mobile application (the "App"). It applies to all users of the App, including Foodie Content Creators ("Creators") and Restaurant partners ("Restaurants").

When you register for the App, you will be presented with a clear, explicit consent step — a mandatory tickbox requiring you to actively confirm that you have read and agree to this Privacy Policy. Your consent is recorded with a timestamp at the point of registration. Without giving this consent, registration cannot be completed. If you do not agree to this Policy, please do not register for or use the App.

CONSENT NOTICE:

Your consent is collected actively at registration via a mandatory tickbox. Your consent timestamp is logged. You may withdraw your consent at any time by contacting us, though this will affect your ability to use the App.

2. Who This Policy Applies To

This Privacy Policy applies to two categories of users:

  • Foodie Content Creators – individuals who register on the App to discover and apply for UGC collaboration opportunities with Restaurants.
  • Restaurants – food and beverage businesses that register on the App to post collaboration briefs, review Creator profiles, and manage UGC campaigns.

All users must be at least 18 years of age. The App is not intended for use by minors. If we become aware that a user is under 18, we will deactivate their account and delete their data.

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • To display your profile to relevant parties (Restaurants viewing Creator profiles and vice versa)
  • To facilitate collaboration matching between Creators and Restaurants
  • To enable in-app messaging between matched users
  • To send push notifications about new collaboration opportunities, messages, and platform updates

5. Legal Basis for Processing Your Data

In accordance with Malaysia's Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA), we process your personal data on the following bases:

  • Consent – You have given explicit, active consent at registration, recorded with a timestamp. You may withdraw this consent at any time, subject to Section 9 below.
  • Contractual necessity – Processing is necessary to fulfil our obligations to you as a user of the App.
  • Legitimate interests – We process certain data (e.g. analytics, security) for our legitimate business interests, where these do not override your rights.
  • Legal obligation – We may process your data where required to comply with applicable law.

  • Standard contractual clauses (data processing agreements) with our cloud service providers

By using the App, you acknowledge that your data will be transferred to and processed in the countries described above, subject to these safeguards.

8. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide you with the App's services.

8.1 Active Accounts

We retain your data for the duration of your account's active status. You may request deactivation of your account at any time by contacting us at share.eat2026@gmail.com.

8.2 Following Deactivation – 12-Month Retention Window

Upon deactivation, your account will be soft-deleted (suspended and no longer accessible to other users). We will retain your personal data for a period of 12 months following deactivation for the following purposes:

  • To resolve any ongoing or potential disputes arising from your use of the App
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations
  • To prevent fraud and enforce our Terms of Service

After this 12-month period, your data will be permanently and irreversibly deleted from our systems, unless we are required by applicable law to retain it for longer. Where law requires longer retention (for example, financial records under Malaysia's Companies Act 2016 may need to be kept for up to 7 years once payments are introduced), we will retain only the minimum data necessary to fulfil that specific legal obligation.

8.3 Requesting Permanent Erasure

Deactivation is not the same as permanent erasure. If you wish to have your personal data permanently deleted before the 12-month window has elapsed, you must submit a separate written erasure request to share.eat2026@gmail.com. We will process erasure requests within 21 calendar days, subject to any overriding legal obligations.

IMPORTANT – Deactivation vs. Erasure:

Deactivating your account suspends it. Your data is automatically and permanently deleted after 12 months. If you want your data deleted sooner, you must submit a separate erasure request by email to share.eat2026@gmail.com. These are two different processes.

9. Your Rights Under Malaysia's PDPA

As a data subject under Malaysia's Personal Data Protection Act 2010, you have the following rights:

  • Right of Access – You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Correction – You may request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent – You may withdraw your consent to the processing of your data at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal. Note that withdrawing consent may affect or prevent your ability to use the App.
  • Right to Limit Processing – You may request that we limit the processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Deactivation – You may request suspension of your account by contacting share.eat2026@gmail.com. Deactivation suspends your account but does not constitute permanent erasure (see Section 8.2 and 8.3).
  • Right to Erasure – You may separately request permanent deletion of your personal data by submitting a written erasure request to share.eat2026@gmail.com. This is distinct from deactivation and must be requested explicitly. We will action erasure requests within 21 calendar days, subject to legal retention obligations.

DEACTIVATION ≠ ERASURE:

Requesting deactivation of your account and requesting erasure of your personal data are two separate actions requiring two separate requests. Please be explicit about which you are requesting when you contact us.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at share.eat2026@gmail.com. We will respond to all requests within 21 calendar days.

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights, privacy, or wellbeing, we commit to notifying affected users within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach. Notification will be delivered via:

Our breach notification will include: the nature of the breach, the categories of data affected, the likely consequences, and the measures we have taken or propose to take to address it. We will also notify the relevant Malaysian regulatory authority as required under applicable law and any forthcoming amendments to the PDPA.

72-HOUR BREACH NOTIFICATION COMMITMENT:
